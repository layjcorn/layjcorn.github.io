ELECTRONICS & CONTROLS

To accurately simulate a commercial retrofit, the entire system is powered by a standard 12V riding mower battery. Buck and boost converters distribute power across the system: stepping down to 5V for the ESP32 microcontroller and 3.3V for the sensor array, and stepping up to 24V for the stepper motor.

The retraction mechanism relies on a NEMA 17 stepper motor driving an 8mm ballscrew. To optimize torque and precise positional control, the motor is driven by a TMC2209 breakout board. Utilizing the UART interface, we disabled the default StealthChop mode in favor of SpreadCycle to maximize pulling force against the 55.7N spring load. Once the electronic rotary latch secures the guard, a limit switch triggers the ESP32 to reverse the motor and disengage the nut.

MECHANICAL DESIGN

The structural foundation of the mockup mower deck was constructed using 3/4" MDF to ensure rigidity and prevent bowing under the dynamic load of the guard deployment. The guard itself was fabricated from a 30" x 5" sheet of 1/8" polycarbonate. While 1/4" aluminum was calculated to be the optimal material for a production environment to resist a 1143N dynamic impact (simulating an adult kick), polycarbonate was selected for the prototype to provide internal visibility of the rail mechanisms during demonstrations.

Vertical travel is guided by 7mm linear rails and ball-bearing carriages. Because the rails required exacting precision to prevent binding during the 46ms drop, the M2 mounting holes were precision-machined using a mill rather than hand-drilled. Custom PETG standoffs, spring housings, and mechanical stops were 3D printed to manage spacing and prevent the carriages from derailing on uneven terrain.

TESTING & ITERATION

Performance was validated using high-speed camera analysis across multiple dynamic scenarios. The mechanical drop time averaged 46ms, well within the required safety threshold. Total system latency—from the moment a human enters the 1.0m detection zone to the guard impacting the ground—averaged 284ms. The system correctly biased toward false positives, safely deploying when a subject walked perpendicular to the mower, and correctly remaining idle when the mower moved away from a stationary subject.

Engineering Iteration: During assembly, the off-center placement of the retraction motor introduced a severe moment, causing the guard to bind (rack) on the linear rails during upward travel. While we temporarily removed the downforce springs to allow the prototype to retract during testing, this failure highlighted a critical design flaw. Future revisions will utilize a centrally mounted belt-drive system or dual synchronized stepper motors to ensure symmetrical load distribution during reset.
